Our Visionaries

Honoring the legendary founders whose uncompromising vision and philanthropic spirit ignited the spark of premier technical education in India.

Founder, B.M.S. Educational Trust

Late Sri. B.M. Sreenivasaiah

Etched permanently in the illustrious history of Karnataka, the name of Late Businayana Mukundadas Sreenivasaiah (BMS) stands as a monumental pillar in the realm of philanthropy. A visionary ahead of his time, he possessed an unwavering belief in empowering human intellect. Recognizing his profound contributions, the Maharaja of Mysore bestowed upon him the prestigious title of Raja Karya Prasaktha in 1946.

It was in that same golden year he established the B.M.S. College of Engineering. Even before the dawn of India's independence, he boldly foresaw the critical urgency for elite technical education to drive the nation forward. The noble ideals and relentless dedication for which Sri. B. M. Sreenivasaiah stood transcend generations, continuing to deeply inspire and guide the inheritors of his extraordinary legacy today.

Donor Trustee, B.M.S. Educational Trust

Late Sri. B. S. Narayan

Following the monumental legacy of Sri. B.M. Sreenivasaiah, his highly dynamic and brilliantly enterprising son, Sri. B. S. Narayan, courageously took over the reins of the institution. Inheriting his father's unyielding passion, the college surged from strength to magnificent strength under his extraordinarily capable guidance.

A true pioneer of modern educational globalization, he was deeply instrumental in weaving international collaborative programs into the fabric of BMSCE. By initiating the training of foreign students beneath the esteemed International Co-operation Division (ICD) and pioneering cross-cultural synthesis programs with the globally renowned Melton Foundation, he secured BMSCE’s irrefutable position as a worldly destination for technical excellence.
